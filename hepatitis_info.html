Hepatitis

Hepatitis is a viral infection that causes inflammation of the liver. There are several different types of Hepatitis, each caused by a different virus. The most common types of Hepatitis are Hepatitis A, Hepatitis B, and Hepatitis C.

Hepatitis A

Hepatitis A is a highly contagious virus that is transmitted through contaminated food or water. It is typically spread when people consume food or water that has been contaminated by feces from an infected person. Symptoms of Hepatitis A include fever, fatigue, loss of appetite, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and yellowing of the skin and whites of the eyes (jaundice). Most people recover from Hepatitis A without treatment, but it can be serious, particularly in older adults or people with other liver problems. A vaccine is available to prevent Hepatitis A.

Hepatitis B

Hepatitis B is a serious and potentially life-threatening virus that is transmitted through blood and other body fluids. It can be transmitted through sexual contact, sharing needles, or from mother to baby during childbirth. Symptoms of Hepatitis B may include fever, fatigue, loss of appetite, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and jaundice. Some people with Hepatitis B may not have any symptoms, but they can still transmit the virus to others. Hepatitis B can lead to chronic infection, which can cause serious health problems such as cirrhosis (scarring of the liver) and liver cancer. A vaccine is available to prevent Hepatitis B.

Hepatitis C

Hepatitis C is a viral infection that is transmitted through contaminated blood. It is a serious and potentially life-threatening virus that can lead to chronic infection and cause serious health problems such as cirrhosis and liver cancer. Many people with Hepatitis C do not have any symptoms, but they can still transmit the virus to others. Hepatitis C is treated with antiviral medications, which can cure the infection in most cases.

Hepatitis D

Hepatitis D is a virus that can only infect people who are already infected with Hepatitis B. It is transmitted through contaminated blood and can cause serious liver damage.

Hepatitis E

Hepatitis E is a virus that is transmitted through contaminated food or water and is most common in developing countries. It is typically self-limiting, meaning that it goes away on its own, but it can be serious, particularly in pregnant women.
